01 Overview

Publishing daily content
manually doesn't scale

ethereum.pk and crypto.pk are Pakistan's dedicated crypto media properties — covering prices, market movements, news, and analysis for a Pakistani audience. In a market where news moves in seconds, publishing a fresh, well-researched, SEO-optimised blog every morning was taking the team the better part of a day. Research, writing, formatting, keyword optimisation, price predictions — each piece was a full workflow in itself.

The brief: automate the entire content pipeline end-to-end. Every morning, without any human starting it, the system should scan everything — crypto news sources, social media, on-chain signals, market data, public sentiment on Twitter — synthesise what matters, and produce a fully formed, SEO-optimised blog post and price prediction ready to publish.

The human's job was reduced to a single action: read the draft, and press publish. Not write, not research, not format, not optimise. Just approve.

02 The Story

Before, during,
and after

Before
A content team doing the work of a machine

Every morning started the same way. Someone would open ten browser tabs — CoinDesk, CoinTelegraph, Twitter, Reddit — and spend an hour just figuring out what had happened overnight. What moved. What people were talking about. What was worth writing about.

Then came the writing. Research notes stitched into a draft. Keywords guessed at. Meta descriptions added as an afterthought. The whole thing formatted manually before being pasted into the CMS.

On a good day, one post was live by early afternoon. On a bad day — a busy news cycle, a team member out — nothing published at all. Consistency, the single most important factor in SEO, was impossible to guarantee.

A full working day to produce one post. And the moment it went live, the news had already moved on.
The shift
The problem wasn't effort. It was architecture.

The team wasn't doing bad work — they were doing human work that shouldn't have required a human. Scraping sources, compiling data, identifying what's trending: these are pattern-recognition tasks that a machine does faster, more comprehensively, and without getting tired.

The real value the team brought — editorial judgement, understanding what a Pakistani crypto audience cares about, knowing when a story is genuinely significant — was buried under six hours of prep work per post.

The decision was to strip every mechanical task out of the human workflow and replace it with an automated pipeline. Not AI writing for the sake of it — but a system designed to do the boring parts perfectly so the humans could focus on the only thing that actually required them: deciding whether to publish.

The goal was never to replace the team. It was to give them back their time for the work only they could do.
After
The system runs. The team reviews. Content ships.

At 6 AM every morning, the pipeline fires without anyone touching it. By the time the first team member opens their laptop, a fully written, SEO-scored blog post and price prediction are sitting in the content queue — sourced from dozens of live data points, written to a consistent standard, and ready to go.

The team's morning now looks completely different. They read the draft. They check the SEO score. If it clears the threshold, they press publish. Ten minutes, start to finish.

No missed days. No 3 PM posts that catch nobody. No posts that never got written because the morning was chaotic. The pipeline doesn't have bad days.

From a full day of production work to a ten-minute morning review — every single day, without exception.

04 The SEO Engine

Not a prompt.
A system.

The SEO optimiser is the most carefully engineered part of the pipeline. It isn't a single AI call with a keyword list — it's a multi-pass review chain, each stage with a specific mandate, built from months of understanding what actually ranks in crypto search.

The output isn't just readable. It's structured to rank — correct heading hierarchy, keyword placement that reads naturally, meta descriptions that drive clicks, internal linking woven in, and readability calibrated for a technically literate but non-expert audience.

Every published post exits the system with an SEO score above a minimum threshold. Posts that don't pass are flagged for human review rather than silently published at lower quality.

01
Keyword extraction & intent mapping
Identifies primary and secondary keywords from the day's scraped data. Maps search intent — informational, price-check, news — and selects the correct content angle to match what people are actually searching for that morning.
02
Structure & heading optimisation
Enforces correct H1/H2/H3 hierarchy. Ensures the primary keyword appears in the title, first 100 words, and at least two subheadings. Checks keyword density stays in the 1.5–2.5% natural range.
03
Meta & snippet generation
Writes a meta title under 60 characters and meta description under 155 characters — both optimised for click-through rate, not just keyword inclusion. Also generates FAQ schema markup for rich result eligibility.
04
Internal link weaving
Cross-references the existing article database and suggests contextually relevant internal links — ensuring every new post strengthens the site's topical authority cluster rather than sitting as an isolated page.
05
Readability pass & score gate
Final pass checks sentence length, passive voice ratio, paragraph density, and Flesch reading ease. If the combined SEO score doesn't clear the threshold, the post is flagged for human edit rather than passed to the database.
