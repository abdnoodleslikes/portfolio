01 Overview

A lead gen team doing
a machine's job

Smile More Solutions is a Florida-based marketing agency that lives and dies by its pipeline. Their lead generation workflow relied on purchasing bulk contact databases — lists that were never quite right, never fully current, and always required hours of manual enrichment before anyone could actually do anything with them.

The team would take a raw list, then manually search for email addresses, verify them one by one, hunt for phone numbers, try to identify who at the company was worth reaching — all before a single outreach message was sent. The work was tedious, inconsistent, and bottlenecked by whoever had time to do it that day.

What was built is an on-demand lead engine: the user types a keyword and a location, sets how deep they want the enrichment to go, and specifies how many leads they need. The system does the rest — scraping, validating, enriching, finding decision-makers, and pushing everything directly into their GoHighLevel CRM, ready to work.

02 The Story

Before, during,
and after

Before
Paying for data they still had to build themselves

The agency's lead workflow started with a purchase — a database subscription, a scraped list, a CSV from some provider. The problem was that no list ever arrived ready to use. Email addresses needed verifying. Phone numbers were missing or wrong. The name on the record was the company, not a person. Nobody knew who at that company was actually worth calling.

So the team would sit down and enrich. They'd Google the business, find a website, look for a contact page, check LinkedIn for employees, try to identify a decision-maker. Then verify the email. Then log it all. Per lead, that process took minutes. Across hundreds of leads per campaign, it consumed days.

Worse, the quality was inconsistent — different team members enriched differently, some went deeper than others, and there was no standard for what "ready" actually meant before a lead went into GHL.

They were paying twice for every lead — once to get the name, and again in labour to make it usable.
The shift
The data exists. The problem was retrieval, not research.

Every piece of information the team was manually hunting for — business emails, phone numbers, LinkedIn profiles, employee names and titles — is publicly available. The bottleneck wasn't that the data didn't exist. It was that retrieving it required a human to go find it every single time.

The reframe was straightforward: build a system that does the retrieval automatically, to a consistent standard, faster than any person could. The user defines what they want — the keyword, the location, how deep to enrich, how many leads — and the system executes against that spec at machine speed.

The decision-maker logic was the key differentiator. Most tools return a company contact. This system goes further — finding up to five people at the business and ranking them by authority, so the highest-value contact always surfaces first. A CEO before a receptionist. A marketing director before a coordinator.

The insight wasn't a new data source. It was automating the exact retrieval steps the team was already doing — just systematically, instantly, and at any scale.
After
A keyword and a location. A fully enriched list in GHL.

Now a campaign starts differently. Someone on the team opens the interface, types the niche and city — "dental clinics, Miami" — sets enrichment depth and lead count, and runs it. The system scrapes matching businesses, validates every email address, pulls phone numbers, finds the LinkedIn profiles of up to five employees per company, ranks them by seniority, and pushes the entire structured dataset into GHL.

What comes out the other end isn't a raw list. It's a contact record with a verified email, a phone number, the business socials, and the names and profiles of the people most likely to say yes — in order of who to call first.

The team went from spending days on enrichment to spending that time on outreach. The quality floor on every lead is now consistent and defined by the system, not by whoever happened to be enriching that day.

From bulk databases that needed days of manual work — to a targeted, fully enriched, decision-maker-first lead list, ready in minutes.
